A Personal, Tested Shortlist

Where I actually go for sound teaching.

Not a list of everything that's out there — a short list of what I've personally checked against Scripture, used over time, and found trustworthy. Anyone can quote a verse. Fewer handle the whole counsel of God with care.

8 resources 0 affiliate links Updated June 2026

Why a curated list, and not just "search it yourself"

A verse lifted out of its paragraph can be made to defend almost anything. That isn't a reason to stop reading — it's a reason to read more carefully, and to be honest about whose teaching you're letting shape you.

I don't share things here because they're popular. I share them because I've sat with them long enough to see how they handle a hard passage, how they treat people they disagree with, and whether they point you back to the text or just to themselves. Test everything here the same way — that's the whole point.

Bible Study Fellowship (BSF)

BSF International

An in-depth, interdenominational Bible study followed alongside others worldwide — in person where a group meets near you, or online, with time zones you can choose to fit your schedule. Studying in a group means your understanding gets tested by other people's questions, not just your own.

For Discernment

Thinking carefully about churches & teachers
Use judgment
YouTube Channel

Heliocentric

Independent reviewer

Reviews of different churches and ministries, looking at doctrine and practice rather than just production value or popularity. I've found the reviews thoughtful and even-handed more often than not.

Why this one comes with a note Any single reviewer — me included — has blind spots. I'm including this because I've found it genuinely useful for raising the right questions, not because one channel should be your only filter. Weigh it against Scripture and against people who actually know you, same as everything else on this page.
Disclaimer The reviewer behind this channel is openly atheist — raised in the church, now visiting and reviewing services from outside the faith rather than as a believer. That doesn't make every observation wrong, but his conclusions about doctrine and what a church "should" believe aren't coming from a place of faith. Use this for noticing red flags in tone and conduct, and bring real questions of theology back to Scripture and to believers you trust.

For Music

Songs that quote the text, not just gesture at it
Scripture set to song
Music Collective

The Porter's Gate

Founded by Isaac & Megan Wardell

An ecumenical collective of songwriters, pastors, and theologians who write congregational songs drawn directly from the Psalms, the prophets, and the Gospels. Their catalog spans Work Songs, Lament Songs, Justice Songs, and Advent Songs, with biblical language shaping nearly every line rather than just the chorus.

Why I trust this one These songs hold up next to an open Bible. You can trace almost every lyric back to a specific passage, which is rare in contemporary worship music and exactly why I keep coming back to it.

How I Decide What Goes Here

The actual filter, not a vibe
Does it stay in context?

I check whether a teaching deals with the whole passage and its surrounding chapters, or just the one verse that's easy to quote.

Does it survive a second look?

I've gone back to each of these more than once, on different passages, before adding it here. One good episode isn't enough.

Can I see the reasoning?

Good teaching shows you how it got to a conclusion. I'm wary of anything that only hands you the conclusion.

Does it point past itself?

I look for teaching that sends you back to your own Bible and your own prayer life — not teaching that wants to become the only voice you listen to.
